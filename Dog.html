Dog Information

Basic information on Man's Best Friend!


Wellness and Vaccines

When it comes to your canine companion, getting them vaccinated is very important. As a young pup, it is especially important to get their vaccines done as soon as they are at the appropriate age, usually starting at 6-weeks-old. This may include a wellness exam, tests for intestinal parasites, and the first boosters for DHPP, DPP, or DAP (includes distemper, adenovirus, hepatitis, parvovirus, parainfluenza, depending on the veterinarian). Next, they should get their rabies vaccine. Rabies vaccines have different time lengths depending on the age of your dog. Additional vaccines, including bordetella, lyme, rattlesnake, influenza and leptospirosis, can be requested if needed.

Neuter and Spay

Neuter and spay surgery is the removal of the animal's ability to reproduce, thus avoiding possible cancer, infections, unintended pregnancy, and behavioral issues. Most vets would suggest having your dog get this surgery when they are six-months-old.

Flea, Tick, and Heartworm and Internal Parasite Prevention

Fleas and ticks are the main parasites owners try to avoid. There are many over-the-counter medications, collars, and shampoos that can relieve your pets from those parasites, as well as prescriptions obtained from a veterinarian. There are also many parasites that could be living inside of your dog. Parasites like hookworms, roundworms, and heartworms can infect a dog from the environment. If your dog goes outside, they are at risk for these parasites, as well as ringworm and mites that can affect the skin. Talk to your veterinarian to determine the right medications to kill and prevent any infestations.

Lifespan & Sleeptime

The lifespan of your canine companion will vary based on breed, size, lifestyle, and medical history. The average lifespan for a small dog under 20 pounds is around 13 years, a medium-sized dog around 11 years, and large dog around 9 years. The amount of sleep a dog will get will again vary on breed, age, size, and lifestyle. The average dog will sleep between 12 and 14 hours per day. Larger dogs, dogs with lower activity levels, puppies and senior dogs will sleep for longer periods of time.

Exercise

As with every person and every animal, exercise is an important part of healthy living for your dog. Every dog is an individual, with individual exercise needs. Puppies, and working and athletic breeds will usually require more exercise. Exercise needs will also change with different personalities. If at any time you doubt your dog's exercise needs being matched, consult with your veterinarian. Obesity in pets is just as serious a disease as in humans.
