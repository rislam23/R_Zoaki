Our Purpose

Around the US, there are around 6.5 million pets going into shelters. Of those 6.5 million, 1.5 million of them are euthanized to make room in those shelters. Around 44 percent of the US own dogs and 29 percent own cats. Millennials between the ages of 20 and 30 are looking for pets more than having a child. With these numbers, our goal is to help present and future pet owners find their future pets. This will decrease the amount of animals being sent to shelter and in turn decrease the amount of euthanization of pets in the shelters. Our objective to create a website that will inform pet owners and provide an “online shelter” that will connect users with animals via the web. The services provided will include providing pet owners with information on how to care for their pet and a platform for adopting animals.

Meet The Team

Coders, programmers, and animal lovers, we are a group of students from Kennesaw State with the goal of reducing animal homelessness. Each group member brings unique talents and skills to help achieve our goal. Phillip Pujada, our animal expert, has owned many animals for years and has combated the problem first hand. He has spent a lot of time volunteering at shelters and is offering valuable insight and information to the project. On the technical side, the rest of the team Troy Wu, John Nnamezie, Myron Woods, and Rafat Islam have many years of programming experience. Experience like John Nnamezie's deep understanding of frameworks and Troy Wu's years of real world back end production. Our team has the skills to make our goal a reality.

We Need You

While we strive toward our goal, you can help. If you or somebody you know are looking for a pet, be sure to check your local shelters. There are many animals that could use a good home. Not only will you provide them with a home, in many cases you will be saving their life. So, show your support and adopt an animal today.
