Rabbit Information

Basic information on your bouncing bunny!


Wellness and Vaccines

Since rabbits require different care from a typical domestic pet, finding a veterinarian that provides services to rabbits and other exotic animals is essential. Rabbits can be vaccinated as early as five-weeks-old with vaccines against myxomatosis and rabbit (viral) haemorrhagic disease. Consult with your veterinarian to determine the best vaccines and wellness care your rabbit needs.

Neuter and Spay

Neuter and spay surgery is the removal of the animal's ability to reproduce, thus avoiding possible cancer, infections, unintended pregnancy, and behavioral issues. Most vets would suggest having your rabbit spayed or neutered at three-and-a-half or four-months-old.

Flea, Tick, and Heartworm and Internal Parasite Prevention

Keeping your rabbit's enclosure clean and free from insects and parasites is the first essential step to preventing infestations. Coccidia, cryptosporidium, pinworms and roundworms are internal parasites that can afflict your rabbit. Fleas and ticks are not picky on what animal they get their meal from, rabbits included. Ask your veterinarian about the topical or oral medicines that will keep your rabbit parasite-free.

Lifespan & Sleeptime

The average lifespan for a domestic rabbit is between eight and twelve years. Keeping your rabbit safe and healthy will help prolong their life. As for sleep, rabbits are most active at dusk and dawn. They sleep about eight hours per day, and will spend the rest of the time going about their individual routine in their enclosure.

Exercise

Each rabbit will have their own personality to match the time they exercise and go about their day. The key for any rabbit owner is to provide an appropriate number of toys and enrichment in their rabbit's enclosure. If you have any concerns regarding your rabbit's activity level and engagement, ask your veterinarian.
